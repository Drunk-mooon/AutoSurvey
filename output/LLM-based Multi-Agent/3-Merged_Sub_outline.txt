## 3 Types of Microbial Enzymes in Pollutant Degradation  
Description: This section will categorize various microbial enzymes relevant to pollutant bioremediation, including hydrolases, oxidoreductases, and transferases, discussing their functions and applications in biochemical pathways.

### 3.1 Hydrolases  
Description: This subsection will discuss hydrolases, a class of enzymes that catalyze the hydrolysis of chemical bonds, detailing their roles in breaking down various pollutants such as plastic and heavy metals.

### 3.2 Oxidoreductases  
Description: This subsection will explore oxidoreductases, enzymes that facilitate oxidation-reduction reactions, highlighting their application in the bioremediation of organic pollutants through redox reactions.

### 3.3 Transferases  
Description: This subsection will categorize transferases, which move functional groups from one molecule to another, and discuss their importance in pollutant degradation pathways.

### 3.4 Lyases  
Description: This subsection will cover lyases, enzymes that cleave chemical bonds by means other than hydrolysis and oxidation, outlining their specific uses in the breakdown of complex environmental contaminants.

### 3.5 Isomerases  
Description: This subsection will describe isomerases, enzymes that catalyze the rearrangement of molecular structures, emphasizing their utility in transforming pollutants into less harmful substances.

### 3.6 Ligases  
Description: This subsection will examine ligases, enzymes that facilitate the joining of two molecules with the consumption of ATP, focusing on their application in reconstructing bioproducts from waste materials.

### 3.7 Enzyme Mixtures and Synergistic Effects  
Description: This subsection will investigate the effectiveness of enzyme mixtures in bioremediation strategies, discussing how different types of microbial enzymes can work synergistically to enhance pollutant degradation.

### 3.8 Enzyme Modifications and Engineering  
Description: This subsection will highlight advancements in enzyme engineering, including genetic modifications that enhance the efficiency, stability, and specificity of microbial enzymes used in bioremediation.
